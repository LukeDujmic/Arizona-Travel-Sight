Peoria Environment and Atmosphere

Peoria offers great vibes in its essence, being located in the northern parts of the Sonoron Desert. Located about 30 minutes northwest of downtown Phoenix, Peoria was established in 1886 as a humble agricultural community. Since incorporating on June 7, 1954, Peoria has blossomed as a rapidly-growing, modern city that offers a high quality of living.

The Landscape

The geography of Peoria mostly reflects the nature of the southern half of Arizona. The land offers a plethora of various hills and mountains that offers trails all over. One of these is Deem Hills, which offers unique rock formations and a generally enjoyable hike. West Wing Mountain is an extroadinary preserve of land which offers a vast array of mountains to explore. It is a dry region, featuring beautiful cacti across the region alonside various rocks. One of the most redeeming features of Peoria are its regional reserves and parks. Lake Pleasant is an astounding body of water, especially in contrast of the dry arid regions of Arizona.

The Neighborhoods

The neighborhoods of Peoria form a peaceful atmosphere. They offer unique structural styles that stand out from other homes. Lawns and backyards stand out from the rest, with distinct wall and path structures that satisfy any style.

The City

The city offers all the features you look for in a nice place to settle. Stores and restaurants are put together in common areas to allow for easy and distinct access. The city is lit all over the place, so there's no concern at night. The streets are well paved and uniformly structured, allowing for easy drives. There are several hotels, like the WigWam or Cibola Vista Resort, which offer beautiful structures that fit into the land around it. Football fields, basketball courts, tennis courts, neighborhood parks, public pools/waterparks are just a few of many features that the city has to offer.
